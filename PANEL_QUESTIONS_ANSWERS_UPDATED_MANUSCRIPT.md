# Panel Questions Answer Bank Based on the Updated Manuscript

This file answers the questions and side comments from `questions of panels.docx` using the updated manuscript, especially the current Project Context, Problem and Solution Statement, Purpose and Description, Scope and Limitations, Requirements-Features Matrix, and Evaluation Plan.

## Main Defense Position

Use this as the safest overall framing:

> LYDO Connect is currently scoped as a Pasig City LYDO/PCYDO implementation. The study uses Metro Manila and Philippine e-governance conditions as background support, but the actual system, testing, and data model are focused on Pasig City. The system is not claiming to replace all LYDO systems nationwide. It is a centralized PWA that supports information access, participation monitoring, transparency records, service requests, reports, and administrative monitoring for the selected LYDO context.

Important clarifications:

- The manuscript says the target implementation site is the Local Youth Development Office in Pasig City.
- Current Pasig LYDO operations are described as fragmented, using Facebook, Google Forms, Google Drive, Microsoft Excel/Google Sheets, and manual printing.
- The PWA has two major interfaces: a public/user platform and an administrative platform.
- Transparency means access to official programs, services, organization information, disclosure documents, financial summaries, compliance records, and audit-supported administrative activity. It is not a corruption investigation.
- The manuscript still needs a stronger official count of youth organizations. A supporting public research list identified 21 publicly verifiable Pasig youth or youth-serving organizations other than SK councils, but the final official count should be confirmed with Pasig PCYDO/LYDO.

## Short Defense Script

> Our study is focused on Pasig City LYDO/PCYDO. We use Metro Manila as background because similar digital governance gaps exist in many LGUs, but our system implementation and testing are for Pasig. Based on public verification, we identified 21 Pasig-based youth or youth-serving organizations other than SK councils, although the official count still needs confirmation from PCYDO/LYDO. The problem is not simply the number of organizations; it is the fragmented workflow. The office currently uses separate tools such as Facebook, Google Forms, Google Drive, Excel or Google Sheets, and manual printing. LYDO Connect enhances this by centralizing information access, program and event registration, participation monitoring, organization records, transparency documents, service requests, reports, and audit logs in one PWA. The system supports monitoring and transparency using official records, but it does not replace LYDO governance functions or perform financial auditing.
