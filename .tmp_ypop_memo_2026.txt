Pursuant to City Ordinance No. 50, Series of 2024, otherwise known as the Pasig City Youth 
Development Code, Article V, Section 18.1, provides for the establishment of the Youth 
Participation Organization Passport (YPOP) in recognition of the active engagement of duly 
registered Youth Organizations (YOs) and Youth-Serving Organizations (YSOs). 
 
Building on this mandate, the YPOP Program is open to all Pasig YORP-Registered 
organizations with active status. Central to this program is the YPOP Card—a passport-sized 
identification tool– that serves as the official record of an organization’s engagement. Through 
this card, YOs and YSOs can earn and accumulate points by participating in government-led 
initiatives or by implementing their own community activities. For the year 2026, these cards 
will be officially distributed to all qualified organizations in the scheduled General Assembly. 
 
The accumulated points shall be monitored, evaluated, and officially tallied by the Pasig City 
Youth Development Office (PCYDO) as the basis for granting incentives to the most active and 
participative organizations in the City. To ensure the program’s success, the following guidelines 
are established: 
 
1.​ The YPOP Card: This shall serve as the official record of the organization’s 
accumulated participation points in City-led events and activities. Each organization 
shall be issued only one (1) passport, which must be maintained and safeguarded by 
the organization’s duly authorized representative. 
1.1 Use of the YPOP Card: The card must be presented to the implementing office for 
the recording and validation of points upon attendance at any activity. 
 
1.2 Attendance and YPOP Points: Each Program, Project, and Activity (PPA) shall 
carry a corresponding point to determine the incentives awarded under the program.

●​ Mandatory Activities – These refer to City-led PPAs issued by the Pasig City 
Youth Development Office or other Pasig LGU offices through a formal 
memorandum. Each YO and YSO is required to have members in attendance 
for these events. 
●​ Invitational Activities – These consist of activities that are not compulsory but 
are extended as invitations from the PCYDO for voluntary participation. 
●​ Partnership Activities – These are coordinated PPAs organized by 
non-government organizations (NGOs), national government agencies 
(NGAs), or other sectors that organizations are encouraged to attend as part 
of broader collaboration efforts. 
Building upon these categories, an organization’s performance is measured through a 
percentage-based scoring system. The total YPOP points shall be computed by 
combining points earned from both City-led and organization-initiated activities as 
follows: 
●​ City-led Activities – Points earned from participation in City-led activities shall 
be calculated as a percentage of the total possible points allocated for these 
events. 
●​ Organization-initiated Activities – Points earned from programs and activities 
independently initiated by the organization shall likewise be calculated as a 
percentage of the total possible points allocated for such initiatives. 
●​ Total Points Calculation – The organization’s final YPOP score shall be the sum 
of the percentage-based points from both City-led and organization-initiated 
activities.

1.3 Timeline of YPOP Card Checking – Every organization’s YPOP Passport shall be 
checked semi-annually to validate attendance in the activities they participated in. 
 
1.4 Loss of the YPOP Card – In the event of the loss of a YPOP Card, the concerned 
organization shall immediately notify the PCYDO by submitting a formal letter. The 
organization must also provide a narrative report along with photo documentation as 
evidence of their participation in events, which will be used for the reconsideration and 
validation of their YPOP points. 
 
1.5 Eligibility for Incentives – YOs/YSOs that have reached the total required 
percentage (70%) based on the total number of City-led activities mandated by the 
PCYDO are eligible for the project grant incentives of the YPOP Program. 
 
1.6 Ineligibility – YOs/YSOs that have not reached the required percentage are not 
eligible for the project grant incentives. 
 
1.7 Organization-initiated Projects – YOs/YSOs that implement their own PPAs will 
be granted additional points on top of those earned from City-led activities.

●​ Post-Activity Report (template will be provided by PCYDO) 
●​ Liquidation Report with official receipts or payrolls (for the honoraria of 
facilitators and speakers) 
●​ Photo Documentation 
●​ Attendance Sheets

3.3 The project must be implemented according to the timeline, objectives, and 
activities as approved. Any changes must be communicated in writing and formally 
approved before execution. 
 
3.4 The grant must be used solely for the implementation of the approved project as 
outlined in the submitted and validated proposal. Re-allocation of excess funds is not 
allowed. The budget breakdown indicated in the proposal must be strictly followed.   
In case the liquidation did not tally, it will still result in discredit to the organization and 
disqualification to the next YPOP Program. 
 
3.5 For transparency, photo documentation of implemented activities must be posted in 
the Facebook pages  of each organization and must be shared with PCYDO for 
verification. In case the organization has no Facebook Page, they can use any personal 
account of their member.  
 
3.6 The grantee must ensure that the project activities adhere to all applicable local 
laws, ordinances, and ethical standards. The organization shall bear full responsibility 
for any violations committed during the project. 
 
3.7 Any misrepresentation of facts, falsification of documents, or failure to comply with 
any of the above terms may lead to blacklisting from future PCYDO opportunities and 
may be subject to legal actions. 
  
4. ​
Budget Requirements, Conditions, and Limitations 
  
For Programs, Projects, and Activities (PPAs) with budget proposal below Php 50,000.00: 
a.​ Qualified Youth and Youth-Serving Organizations proposing a PPA with a total 
budget not exceeding Fifty Thousand Pesos may implement their activity for 
a duration of one (1) to two (2) days, depending on the nature and scope of 
the PPA. 
 
b.​ PPAs under this budget range must have a minimum of thirty (30) participants 
and may accommodate up to fifty (50) participants, unless otherwise justified 
and approved by the implementing office. 
 
 


--- PAGE 6 ---
 
 
 
 
c. ​ The proposed budget must be clearly itemized and cost-efficient, reflecting 
the scale of the activity and ensuring that expenses are reasonable, necessary, 
and directly related to the objectives of the PPA. 
 
d.​ Allowable expenses may include but are not limited to, program materials, 
meals and snacks, honoraria, minor venue requirements, and other essential 
costs needed for implementation. Non-essential or unrelated expenses shall not 
be allowed. 
 
For Programs, Projects, and Activities (PPAs) with budget proposal Php 50,000.00 and above: 
a.​ All qualified Youth and Youth-Serving Organizations are entitled to implement 
one (1) (PPA) with a maximum funding allocation of One Hundred Thousand 
Pesos (Php 100,000.00). 
 
b.​ PPAs with a proposed budget exceeding Fifty Thousand Pesos (Php 
50,000.00) must be implemented over a period of three or more days. 
 
c. ​ For PPAs with budgets exceeding Php 50,000.00, the activity must have a 
minimum of fifty (50) participants and may accommodate up to a 
maximum of one hundred (100) participants. 
 
d.​ All budget proposals shall be subject to evaluation and approval in 
accordance with existing guidelines and availability of funds. 
 
e.​ Any modification to the activity duration, or participant number must be 
formally requested in writing and approved by PCYDO prior to 
implementation.
