# 3.0 Methodology Overview

This study follows a design-and-development approach in creating **LYDO Connect**, a web-based Youth Information, Engagement, and Transparency Management System intended to support youth information access, program and event participation, citizen concern handling, and administrative transparency functions. The methodology section gives a concise overview of how the study will be conducted, from identifying requirements up to developing and evaluating the proposed system.

The study adopts a **hybrid Design Science Research (DSR) and Rapid Application Development (RAD)** approach within the system development life cycle. DSR is used to guide problem identification, artifact design, and solution relevance, while RAD supports iterative prototyping, module refinement, and faster development of both the public portal and admin portal. At a high level, the process covers requirements analysis, use case and data modeling, system design, implementation, testing, and evaluation.

Data gathering for the study will be supported by stakeholder interviews, document review, consultation with intended users or administrators, and evaluation instruments such as surveys or assessment forms for testing the developed system. This overview is intentionally brief and high-level, since the detailed procedures, diagrams, tools, testing methods, system requirements, quality plans, and evaluation activities are discussed in the succeeding sections of Chapter 3.
