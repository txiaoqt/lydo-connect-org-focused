# 3.0 Methodology

This section presents the methodology of the study for the development of **LYDO Connect**, a web-based Youth Information, Engagement, and Transparency Management System. It provides a concise discussion of how the study will be conducted, including the research design, the SDLC model, the data-gathering methods, and the system development approach that will guide the creation and evaluation of the proposed system. The discussion is intentionally high-level, while the more detailed procedures are presented in the succeeding parts of the proposal.

## Research Design

The study adopts a **descriptive-developmental research design**. It is descriptive because it gathers and analyzes the needs, experiences, and assessments of the intended users and stakeholders of LYDO Connect, such as youth users, citizens, LYDO personnel, staff, and evaluators. It is developmental because the study focuses on designing, building, refining, and evaluating an information system that addresses identified problems in youth information management, participation services, transparency publication, citizen concern handling, policy agreement tracking, and administrative accountability.

To strengthen this design, the study is guided by **Design Science Research (DSR)** and **Rapid Application Development (RAD)**. DSR provides the research structure for identifying the problem, defining the objectives of the solution, building the system artifact, and evaluating its usefulness. RAD supports iterative prototyping and continuous refinement of the system's modules, interfaces, and workflows.

## SDLC Model

The study follows a **modified System Development Life Cycle (SDLC)** using the **RAD model**. This model is appropriate because LYDO Connect consists of multiple modules that require rapid prototyping, repeated revisions, and feedback-based improvement. At a high level, the SDLC process includes planning and requirements analysis, system design, prototype development, database and application construction, testing, refinement, and final evaluation. This approach allows the researchers to improve both the public portal and the admin portal while maintaining alignment with the goals of the study. The current implementation supports separate or combined deployment surfaces for user-facing and admin-facing access, which fits the iterative development approach because each surface can be verified according to its own workflow.

## Data-Gathering Methods

The study will use both **primary** and **secondary** data sources. Primary data will be gathered through stakeholder interviews, consultations, and evaluation surveys or assessment forms involving intended users, administrators, staff, and qualified evaluators. These data-gathering activities will help identify functional needs, usability concerns, and quality expectations for the system.

Secondary data will be gathered through document review and examination of existing records, policies, public information materials, and related system references relevant to LYDO operations, transparency requirements, youth programs, youth organizations, citizen services, and platform terms or privacy requirements. These sources will help define the content, workflows, and records that the system must support, including verified organization profiles with official source references, public transparency records, registration records, and policy acceptance records.

## System Development Approach

The system development approach combines **DSR as the research framework** and **RAD as the software development strategy**. Through DSR, the study will identify the problem context, define solution objectives, develop the system artifact, demonstrate its functionality, and evaluate its effectiveness. Through RAD, the study will create working prototypes, gather feedback, revise forms and workflows, and refine the system through short iterative cycles.

For LYDO Connect, this approach is suitable because the system is not limited to a single function. It integrates user account access, active Terms of Service and Privacy Policy agreement, youth record management, program and event registration workflows, Google Forms/Sheets registration sync support, verified organization information viewing, transparency publication, citizen ticket handling, registration monitoring, admin dashboards, and audit logging. A hybrid DSR-RAD approach therefore allows the study to remain research-driven while also supporting practical and flexible system development.

## Summary

Overall, the methodology of the study is anchored on a descriptive-developmental design, a modified SDLC under the RAD model, stakeholder-based data gathering, and a hybrid DSR-RAD development approach. This methodology is appropriate for LYDO Connect because it supports both the structured development of the proposed system and the evaluation of its relevance, usability, and quality.
