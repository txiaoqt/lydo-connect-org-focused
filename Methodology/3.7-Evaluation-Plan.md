# 3.7 Evaluation Plan

The purpose of this evaluation is to assess the effectiveness, efficiency, usability, reliability, security, and overall acceptability of LYDO Connect as a web-based organization registration, compliance, budget monitoring, liquidation, YPOP validation, and administrative support platform. The evaluation will determine whether the system supports the needs of organization users, LYDO administrators, management representatives, and evaluators while maintaining accurate records, policy agreement handling, role-based access, workflow monitoring, notification support, and audit-oriented administrative processes.

## 3.7.1 Evaluation Activities

a. User Acceptance Testing (UAT): Representatives from the intended user groups, such as organization users, LYDO administrators, management representatives, and system evaluators, will participate in UAT sessions. These sessions will assess whether the major workflows are understandable, complete, and acceptable for actual use. The scenarios may include login, policy agreement acceptance, organization profile completion, compliance submission, budget request submission, liquidation reporting, YPOP submission, notification review, and selected administrative tasks.

b. Functional Workflow Testing: The system will be evaluated against its documented functional requirements to verify that each major feature works as intended. This includes authentication, policy acceptance, organization profile management, compliance document submission, budget request handling, liquidation workflow processing, YPOP record handling, YORP-related organization registry support, template management, news release management, notification handling, and activity log review.

c. Registration and Compliance Review: Organization registration and compliance workflows will be examined to confirm that user-submitted records are stored correctly, duplicate or invalid submissions are handled properly, and review-related remarks or status updates appear correctly in user and admin views. Administrative review features, including verification, revision requests, rejection handling, and protected approval actions, will also be checked.

d. Document and Records Analysis: An examination of system records will be conducted to verify the accuracy and completeness of stored information. This includes organization profiles, compliance document submissions, budget requests, liquidation reports, YPOP records, YORP-related organization fields, template records, notifications, and activity logs. The review will help determine whether system information is traceable, understandable, and consistent with the intended governance and workflow-monitoring purpose of the platform.

e. User Feedback Surveys: Structured feedback surveys will be administered to gather user perception of the system. Respondents will evaluate the system based on functional suitability, usability, performance efficiency, reliability, security, maintainability, and overall acceptability. Open-ended items may also be included to collect comments on user experience, encountered issues, and suggested improvements.

f. Performance Metrics: Key performance indicators will be observed to measure the efficiency of the system during representative tasks. These may include page loading behavior, record retrieval speed, responsiveness of search and filtering, upload and preview behavior, budget monitoring responsiveness, notification loading, and the ability of the system to handle common organization-user and administrator workflows without noticeable delay.

g. Security and Access Control Assessment: Security-related behavior will be reviewed to ensure that protected features are accessible only to authorized users. The assessment will include role-based redirection, admin route protection, policy agreement enforcement for authenticated non-admin users, validation of user-submitted data, secure handling of workflow records, and logging of important administrative changes.

h. Auditability and Accountability Review: Administrative actions will be examined to determine whether changes to important records are traceable. Activity logs will be reviewed for actor information, affected record, operation type, timestamp, and related workflow context when available. This activity will help verify that LYDO Connect supports accountability in organization registration, compliance, finance, and YPOP-related workflows.

## 3.7.2 Respondents and Participants

The evaluation will use purposive sampling because the respondents must be connected to the intended use of the system. The participants may include organization users who can evaluate the user portal, LYDO administrators or staff personnel who can evaluate administrative workflows, and IT experts, faculty members, or system evaluators who can assess technical quality. LYDO management representatives may also be included when monitoring and oversight functions need to be evaluated. The final number of respondents will depend on the actual study coverage, availability of participants, and adviser requirements.

## 3.7.3 Evaluation Instrument

The primary evaluation instrument will be a structured questionnaire using a five-point Likert scale. The questionnaire items may be based on software quality characteristics such as functional suitability, performance efficiency, compatibility, usability, reliability, security, maintainability, and portability. The instrument will also include workflow-specific items for policy agreement handling, organization profile completion, compliance submission, budget processing, liquidation reporting, YPOP validation, notification clarity, monitoring summaries, and activity-log traceability.

Open-ended questions may be added to allow respondents to provide comments, report difficulties, and suggest improvements. These responses will be used to support the interpretation of the quantitative results and identify possible revisions.

## 3.7.4 Data Gathering Procedure

The data gathering procedure will be conducted as follows:

1. Secure permission from the adviser, target office, and participants.
2. Prepare the evaluation questionnaire, task guide, and test scenarios.
3. Demonstrate the main modules of LYDO Connect to the participants.
4. Allow participants to perform representative tasks based on their role.
5. Administer the evaluation questionnaire after the system demonstration or hands-on testing.
6. Collect the completed questionnaires and organize the responses.
7. Summarize the quantitative ratings and qualitative comments.
8. Identify issues, recommendations, and possible system improvements.

## 3.7.5 Data Analysis Plan

The collected responses will be analyzed using descriptive statistics. Frequency counts may be used to summarize respondent profiles, while the weighted mean will be used to determine the level of acceptability for each evaluation criterion. Standard deviation may also be used to observe the consistency of responses. Written comments will be analyzed through qualitative synthesis to identify recurring concerns, strengths, and suggestions.

| Weighted Mean Range | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| 4.21 - 5.00 | Excellent / Highly Acceptable |
| 3.41 - 4.20 | Very Good / Acceptable |
| 2.61 - 3.40 | Good / Moderately Acceptable |
| 1.81 - 2.60 | Fair / Needs Improvement |
| 1.00 - 1.80 | Poor / Not Acceptable |

## 3.7.6 Acceptance Criteria

LYDO Connect may be considered acceptable if the major workflow tests are completed successfully, the evaluation results fall within the acceptable or highly acceptable range, and expert evaluators do not identify unresolved critical issues. The system should also demonstrate understandable navigation, accurate organization and workflow records, successful compliance and budget monitoring behavior, proper policy agreement handling, working liquidation and YPOP processes, protected admin access, and traceable activity logs.
