# 3.7 Evaluation Plan

The purpose of this evaluation is to assess the effectiveness, efficiency, usability, reliability, security, and overall acceptability of LYDO Connect as a web-based youth governance, transparency, program/event registration, and youth service platform. The evaluation will determine whether the system supports the needs of youth users, admin and staff personnel, and evaluators while maintaining accurate records, accessible public information, role-based access, policy agreement handling, registration monitoring, and audit-supported administrative workflows.

## 3.7.1 Evaluation Activities

a. User Acceptance Testing (UAT): Representatives from the intended user groups, such as youth users, admin and staff personnel, and system evaluators, will participate in UAT sessions. These sessions will assess whether the major workflows are understandable, complete, and acceptable for actual use. The scenarios may include account registration, login, policy agreement acceptance, program or event browsing, program or event registration, profile viewing, youth desk ticket submission, transparency viewing, and selected administrative tasks.

b. Functional Workflow Testing: The system will be evaluated against its documented functional requirements to verify that each major feature works as intended. This includes public portal navigation, program and event records, organization information viewing, source reference inspection, registration validation, youth desk tracking, transparency records, financial disclosure, barangay map data, user management, role handling, and audit log review.

c. Registration and Participation Review: Program and event registration workflows will be examined to confirm that user-submitted records are stored correctly, duplicate or invalid submissions are handled properly, and participation history appears in the user profile. Admin monitoring features, including filtering, CSV export, external sheet preview, sync status, failed sync retry, and protected sync-run actions, will also be checked when applicable.

d. Document and Records Analysis: An examination of system records will be conducted to verify the accuracy and completeness of stored information. This includes transparency documents, financial records, compliance board entries, monthly compliance records, organization profiles, source-backed references, barangay data, and youth service tickets. The review will help determine whether public-facing information is traceable, understandable, and consistent with the intended governance and transparency purpose of the system.

e. User Feedback Surveys: Structured feedback surveys will be administered to gather user perception of the system. Respondents will evaluate the system based on functional suitability, usability, performance efficiency, reliability, security, maintainability, and overall acceptability. Open-ended items may also be included to collect comments on user experience, encountered issues, and suggested improvements.

f. Performance Metrics: Key performance indicators will be observed to measure the efficiency of the system during representative tasks. These may include page loading behavior, record retrieval speed, responsiveness of search and filtering, registration submission time, admin table loading time, CSV export behavior, and the ability of the system to handle common user and administrator workflows without noticeable delay.

g. Security and Access Control Assessment: Security-related behavior will be reviewed to ensure that protected features are accessible only to authorized users. The assessment will include role-based redirection, admin route protection, policy agreement enforcement for authenticated non-admin users, validation of user-submitted data, secure handling of service requests, and audit logging of administrative changes.

h. Auditability and Accountability Review: Administrative actions will be examined to determine whether changes to important records are traceable. Audit logs will be reviewed for actor information, affected table or record, operation type, timestamp, and changed values when available. This activity will help verify that LYDO Connect supports accountability in youth governance and transparency workflows.

## 3.7.2 Respondents and Participants

The evaluation will use purposive sampling because the respondents must be connected to the intended use of the system. The participants may include youth users who can evaluate the public portal, admin and staff personnel who can evaluate administrative workflows, and IT experts, faculty members, or system evaluators who can assess technical quality. The final number of respondents will depend on the actual study coverage, availability of participants, and adviser requirements.

## 3.7.3 Evaluation Instrument

The primary evaluation instrument will be a structured questionnaire using a five-point Likert scale. The questionnaire items may be based on software quality characteristics such as functional suitability, performance efficiency, compatibility, usability, reliability, security, maintainability, and portability. The instrument will also include workflow-specific items for policy agreement, program/event registration, organization information viewing, transparency records, youth desk tracking, registration monitoring, role-based access, and audit log review.

Open-ended questions may be added to allow respondents to provide comments, report difficulties, and suggest improvements. These responses will be used to support the interpretation of the quantitative results and identify possible revisions.

## 3.7.4 Data Gathering Procedure

The data gathering procedure will be conducted as follows:

1. Secure permission from the adviser, target office, and participants.
2. Prepare the evaluation questionnaire, task guide, and test scenarios.
3. Demonstrate the main modules of LYDO Connect to the participants.
4. Allow participants to perform representative tasks based on their role.
5. Administer the evaluation questionnaire after the system demonstration or hands-on testing.
6. Collect the completed questionnaires and organize the responses.
7. Summarize the quantitative ratings and qualitative comments.
8. Identify issues, recommendations, and possible system improvements.

## 3.7.5 Data Analysis Plan

The collected responses will be analyzed using descriptive statistics. Frequency counts may be used to summarize respondent profiles, while the weighted mean will be used to determine the level of acceptability for each evaluation criterion. Standard deviation may also be used to observe the consistency of responses. Written comments will be analyzed through qualitative synthesis to identify recurring concerns, strengths, and suggestions.

| Weighted Mean Range | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| 4.21 - 5.00 | Excellent / Highly Acceptable |
| 3.41 - 4.20 | Very Good / Acceptable |
| 2.61 - 3.40 | Good / Moderately Acceptable |
| 1.81 - 2.60 | Fair / Needs Improvement |
| 1.00 - 1.80 | Poor / Not Acceptable |

## 3.7.6 Acceptance Criteria

LYDO Connect may be considered acceptable if the major workflow tests are completed successfully, the evaluation results fall within the acceptable or highly acceptable range, and expert evaluators do not identify unresolved critical issues. The system should also demonstrate understandable navigation, accurate public records, successful registration and monitoring behavior, proper policy agreement handling, working youth desk tracking, reliable transparency and financial record viewing, protected admin access, and traceable audit logs.
