# 3.7 Evaluation Plan

The evaluation plan explains how LYDO Connect will be assessed after development. Since the study is artifact-oriented, the evaluation should determine whether the system is functional, usable, relevant, and acceptable to its intended users and evaluators.

## 3.7.1 Evaluation Method

The system may be evaluated using a combination of:

- task-based system demonstration;
- expert review of technical and functional adequacy; and
- user evaluation based on software quality criteria such as ISO/IEC 25010.

This approach is appropriate because it captures both technical correctness and user perception.

## 3.7.2 Research Instrument

The main evaluation instrument may be a structured questionnaire using a five-point Likert scale. Items may be grouped under the following dimensions:

- functional suitability;
- performance efficiency;
- compatibility;
- usability;
- reliability;
- security;
- maintainability; and
- portability.

Open-ended comment items may also be added to gather improvement suggestions from respondents.

## 3.7.3 Population and Sampling

For this study, **purposive sampling** is more appropriate than random sampling because the respondents should come from the actual stakeholder groups of the system. The respondents may include:

- youth users or citizens who can interact with the public portal;
- LYDO administrators or staff who can review the admin workflow; and
- IT experts, faculty members, or system evaluators who can assess technical quality.

A practical composition may include one group of intended end users and one smaller group of expert evaluators. The final respondent count should be adjusted to the actual study coverage and adviser requirement.

## 3.7.4 Data Gathering Procedure

The data gathering process may follow these steps:

1. Secure permission from the adviser, target office, and respondents.
2. Prepare the evaluation instrument and task guide.
3. Demonstrate the main system modules to the respondents.
4. Allow respondents to perform representative tasks or explore the system.
5. Administer the evaluation questionnaire.
6. Collect completed forms and organize the data for analysis.
7. Summarize comments, issues, and recommendations for revision.

## 3.7.5 Data Analysis Plan

The collected responses may be analyzed using:

- frequency counts for respondent profile summaries;
- weighted mean for each evaluation criterion;
- standard deviation for response consistency; and
- qualitative synthesis of written comments for improvement insights.

### Suggested Likert Interpretation

| Weighted Mean Range | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| 4.21 - 5.00 | Excellent / Highly Acceptable |
| 3.41 - 4.20 | Very Good / Acceptable |
| 2.61 - 3.40 | Good / Moderately Acceptable |
| 1.81 - 2.60 | Fair / Needs Improvement |
| 1.00 - 1.80 | Poor / Not Acceptable |

## 3.7.6 Statistical Treatment and Decision Criteria

For a design-and-development study such as this, descriptive statistics are usually sufficient. Weighted mean and standard deviation are appropriate for determining the level of acceptability of the system. Inferential tests such as `t-test` or `z-test` should only be used if the final research design specifically compares groups and the adviser requires that analysis.

The system may be considered acceptable if:

- the major workflow tests are successful;
- the evaluation results fall within the acceptable or highly acceptable range;
- expert reviewers do not identify unresolved critical issues; and
- respondent comments indicate practical usefulness for the intended context.

## Summary

This evaluation plan fits LYDO Connect better than the template's gaming-business respondent setup because it uses stakeholder groups that are actually connected to youth governance, transparency, and citizen service workflows.
