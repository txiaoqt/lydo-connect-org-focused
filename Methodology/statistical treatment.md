# Statistical Treatment

This section presents the statistical methods used to interpret and summarize the data gathered for **LYDO Connect: A Progressive Web Application for Information, Participation, and Transparency for Local Youth Development Offices**. In alignment with the manuscript and the implemented site workflows, the study uses **descriptive statistics** as the primary treatment of quantitative evaluation data.

The statistical treatment focuses on:
- Frequency
- Percentage
- Mean/Weighted Mean for Likert-scale indicators

Frequency and percentage are used to describe respondent profiles and summarize how many respondents selected each response option for each item. These measures provide the distribution of answers across user and evaluator groups.

The mean/weighted mean is used to determine the level of agreement for each indicator in the 5-point Likert-scale instruments. The resulting scores are interpreted using verbal descriptions to determine the level of system acceptability and perceived performance.

The following formulas were applied:

```text
Percentage (%) = (f / N) x 100
```

Where:
- `f` = frequency of responses
- `N` = total number of respondents

```text
Weighted Mean (WM) = (SUM(f * w)) / N
```

Where:
- `f` = frequency of responses per scale value
- `w` = weight of each scale value
- `N` = total number of responses

For Likert-scale items, the response scale used was:
- 5 = Strongly Agree
- 4 = Agree
- 3 = Neutral
- 2 = Disagree
- 1 = Strongly Disagree

The weighted mean results were interpreted using this guide:

| Weighted Mean Range | Verbal Interpretation |
|---|---|
| 4.21 - 5.00 | Strongly Agree / Highly Acceptable |
| 3.41 - 4.20 | Agree / Acceptable |
| 2.61 - 3.40 | Neutral / Moderately Acceptable |
| 1.81 - 2.60 | Disagree / Needs Improvement |
| 1.00 - 1.80 | Strongly Disagree / Not Acceptable |

## Application to Study Instruments

For the **user-side evaluation instrument**, item means are computed and summarized by major dimensions such as Information Quality, System Quality, Service Quality, post-use behavior, and user satisfaction.

For the **admin/expert evaluation instrument**, item means are computed and summarized by ISO/IEC 25010-aligned criteria (such as functional suitability, usability, performance efficiency, reliability, security, and related quality areas used in the tool).

For interpretation, item-level weighted means are computed first, then summarized per dimension/domain. The resulting values are compared against the verbal interpretation ranges to determine whether each area is acceptable or needs improvement.

## Scope of Statistical Treatment

Consistent with the current proposal manuscript, the primary statistical treatment is descriptive and is intended to summarize evaluation outcomes clearly for Chapter 4 reporting. Qualitative responses from open-ended items are analyzed separately through thematic grouping and are used to explain or support quantitative results.

In summary, this study uses frequency, percentage, and mean/weighted mean as the core statistical treatment of survey data to evaluate LYDO Connect in terms of usability, performance, reliability, security, and overall acceptability in its public and administrative workflows.
