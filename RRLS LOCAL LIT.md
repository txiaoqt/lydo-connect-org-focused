## 1) Digital Platforms + Youth Engagement (Users / Who Is Involved)

### 1.1 Republic Act No. 10742 (2016)
- **Type:** National legal/policy literature
- **Relevance:** Establishes the Sangguniang Kabataan reform framework, including the creation of the **Local Youth Development Council (LYDC)** and **Local Youth Development Office (LYDO)**.  
- **Why it matters to the study:** Defines the formal youth-governance actors, participation structures, and local institutional responsibilities that your system must support.
- **Link:** https://lawphil.net/statutes/repacts/ra2016/ra_10742_2016.html

### 1.2 DILG Memorandum Circular No. 2017-36
- **Type:** National implementation guideline literature
- **Relevance:** Provides implementation direction on the creation of LYDOs at provincial, city, and municipal levels.
- **Why it matters to the study:** Grounds the system in actual local-government implementation requirements, not only statutory intent.
- **Link:** https://region12.dilg.gov.ph/issuances/creation-local-youth-development-office-lydoat-province-city-and-municipal-levels

### 1.3 Medina-Guce, Velasco, & Rey (2025) – PIDS Discussion Paper
- **Type:** Local governance policy literature (baseline/metrics)
- **Relevance:** Examines participation quality in LGU participatory structures and identifies issues in substantive participation.
- **Why it matters to the study:** Supports the need for better participation monitoring and evidence-based tracking mechanisms.
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.62986/dp2025.07

### 1.4 Medina-Guce, Velasco, & Rey (2025) – Philippine Journal of Development
- **Type:** Local governance policy literature
- **Relevance:** Presents baseline findings and recommendations for improving participatory processes in Local Development Councils.
- **Why it matters to the study:** Reinforces that participation effectiveness depends on process quality and institutional design, which your platform aims to operationalize.
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.62986/pjd2025.49.2c

## 2) E-Governance + Transparency (Institutional Need / Existing Problem)

### 2.1 Republic Act No. 12254 (2025) – E-Governance Act
- **Type:** National legal/policy literature
- **Relevance:** Institutionalizes government transition to integrated e-governance and citizen-centered online services.
- **Why it matters to the study:** Serves as direct legal backdrop for platform-based local digital service delivery, transparency, and systems integration.
- **Link:** https://lawphil.net/statutes/repacts/ra2025/ra_12254_2025.html

### 2.2 Siar (2007) – Philippine Journal of Development
- **Type:** Local e-governance assessment literature
- **Relevance:** Assesses city-government websites and reports minimal e-governance adoption and underutilized digital channels.
- **Why it matters to the study:** Documents long-standing LGU digital-governance gaps that remain relevant in platform integration discussions.
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.62986/pjd2005.32.2d

### 2.3 Iglesias (2010) – PIDS Discussion Paper
- **Type:** Local e-governance implementation literature
- **Relevance:** Reviews e-government initiatives of four Philippine cities and maturity of ICT use in local governance.
- **Why it matters to the study:** Provides practical local evidence that governance value increases when systems move from isolated automation to integrated service and citizen relationship models.
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.62986/dp2010.22

### 2.4 Capuno & Garcia (2009) – UPSE Discussion Paper
- **Type:** Local transparency-governance literature
- **Relevance:** Links transparency in local performance disclosure to citizen trust outcomes.
- **Why it matters to the study:** Supports transparency modules and public-facing governance information as trust-building mechanisms.
- **SERP Link:** https://serp-p.pids.gov.ph/publication/public/view?slug=earning-trust-with-transparency-performance-ratings-and-trust-in-local-officials-in-the-philippines

### 2.5 Ramos & Ballesteros (2026) – PIDS Discussion Paper
- **Type:** Contemporary local e-governance literature
- **Relevance:** Assesses e-governance and revenue outcomes in Philippine cities and identifies integration/functionality constraints.
- **Why it matters to the study:** Provides current institutional evidence that digital systems must be functional, integrated, and process-ready to deliver measurable local governance benefits.
- **Link:** https://www.pids.gov.ph/publication/discussion-papers/relevance-of-e-governance-in-revenue-generation-among-philippine-cities

## 3) PWAs + Accessibility (Technological Solution)

### 3.1 PSA + DICT (2025) – 2024 NICTHS Official Release
- **Type:** National ICT access literature/statistical baseline
- **Relevance:** Reports internet access levels, usage patterns, and access barriers across regions and user groups.
- **Why it matters to the study:** Provides empirical local context for accessibility-first system design and cross-device implementation decisions.
- **Link:** https://psa.gov.ph/content/percentage-households-internet-connection-increased-488-percent-2024-two-every-three

### 3.2 Bokingkito et al. (2025)
- **Type:** Local accessibility-focused digital government literature
- **Relevance:** Evaluates accessibility and web presence challenges in Philippine e-government websites.
- **Why it matters to the study:** Supports inclusion requirements, accessibility audits, and usability standards for public-facing government platforms.
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.3390/informatics12020041

### 3.3 Mendoza et al. (2024)
- **Type:** Local mobile digital government accessibility literature
- **Relevance:** Investigates accessibility factors affecting use of the eGovPH mobile application from the citizen perspective.
- **Why it matters to the study:** Useful for framing mobile-first access, user barriers, and interaction quality—key factors when justifying a progressive web application approach.
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.3390/su16030992
