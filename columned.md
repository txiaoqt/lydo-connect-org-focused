# Information, Participation, and Transparency for LYDOs

## Study Context

This framework is intended for the study of common operational issues in **information**, **participation**, and **transparency** among Local Youth Development Offices (LYDOs) in Metro Manila.

## Refined Column Definitions

| Column | Refined Definition |
| --- | --- |
| **Information** | Refers to official youth-related content managed, published, or made accessible by the LYDO through its system. |
| **Participation** | Refers to the involvement of youth individuals or youth organizations in programs, events, activities, and initiatives coordinated, supported, or monitored by the LYDO, including the administrative processes of recording, tracking, organizing, and monitoring such involvement. |
| **Transparency** | Refers to the visibility and accessibility of LYDO processes, requirements, timelines, status updates, decisions, and authorized public-facing records so stakeholders can understand how programs and services are handled. |

## Rewritten Column Content

### Information

**Problem:** fragmented information access

**User (view-only)**

- announcements
- programs and events
- LYDO services
- organization information
- contact details and office information
- public advisories, forms, and guides
- downloadable public resources

**Admin**

- creating and posting announcements
- updating program and event details
- managing service information
- managing organization information
- updating office information and public notices
- publishing downloadable public resources

### Participation

**Problem:** manual or disconnected recording, tracking, organizing, and monitoring of involvement

**User**

- registering for programs and events
- submitting participation or registration forms
- viewing participation history
- confirming attendance or participation where applicable
- submitting organization-related participation records where applicable

**Admin**

- managing participant records
- reviewing registrations or submissions
- tracking attendance and participation
- monitoring organization involvement in activities
- validating organization-related submissions where applicable
- generating participation summaries and reports

### Transparency

**Problem:** limited visibility of processes, requirements, status updates, and public records

**User (view-only)**

- viewing application, request, or registration status
- viewing eligibility, requirements, and deadlines
- viewing processing steps and expected timelines
- viewing official updates, public notices, and announcements
- viewing accomplishment reports, summaries, and other public-facing records

**Admin**

- publishing requirements, criteria, and deadlines
- updating request, application, or registration status
- posting official updates and public notices
- publishing accomplishment reports and summaries
- maintaining accessible public-facing records and archives

## Explanation of the Classification

The three columns should be separated based on their main purpose.

- **Information** is about what the LYDO makes available for people to read, access, or download.
- **Participation** is about actual involvement in LYDO-led or LYDO-monitored programs and the internal management of that involvement.
- **Transparency** is about making LYDO procedures, requirements, progress, and records visible so users can understand what is happening and what to expect.

This means some functions should not be mixed across columns.

- `registering for programs and events` belongs under **Participation** because it is part of joining an activity.
- `managing participant records` also belongs under **Participation** because it deals with tracking involvement.
- `checking request or registration status` fits better under **Transparency** because it gives visibility into process progress.
- `submitting service-related requests` does not fit neatly under these three columns alone; it is closer to **service delivery** or **service access** than to participation or transparency.
